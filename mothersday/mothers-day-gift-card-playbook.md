### 1.7 Fix RSA Ad Strength (Currently "Poor" on All 3)

This is the single most impactful change you can make right now. Google suppresses impressions for Poor-strength ads.

**Why they're rated Poor:**

1. Too many headlines say the same thing differently ("Buy Gift Cards Online," "Order Gift Cards Now," "Gift Cards Available Instantly" — these are all the same message)
2. Descriptions overlap in meaning
3. Not enough variety in headline themes

**How to fix — aim for 12-15 headlines and 4 descriptions per RSA, with headlines organized by theme:**

**Theme categories for headlines (mix at least 5 themes per RSA):**

| Theme | Example Headlines |
|---|---|
| Occasion/urgency | Mother's Day At Spavia, Pamper Mom This Year, She Deserves A Spa Day |
| Product | Massage & Facial Gift Cards, Spa Packages From $238, Signature Massage Gift Card |
| Action/CTA | Buy Gift Cards Online, Order Now & Email Instantly, Shop Gift Cards Today |
| Trust/authority | Voted Best Spa By Plano Magazine, Award-Winning Day Spa, 5-Star Rated Day Spa |
| Local | Spavia Day Spa West Plano, Plano's Top-Rated Day Spa, Polo Towne Crossing |
| Value/price | Gift Cards Starting At $50, Spa Packages From $238, Affordable Luxury |
| Benefit | Mom Picks Her Own Treatment, Valid For Any Service, No Expiration Date |

**Description guidance:**
- Description 1: Lead with the occasion + what you're offering
- Description 2: Specifics (what the gift card covers, how delivery works)
- Description 3: Trust signal (award, location, experience)
- Description 4: Urgency or convenience angle

**Pinning strategy:**
- Pin a keyword-relevant headline to Position 1 (e.g., "Mother's Day Spa Gift Cards" for the Spa Gift Card ad group)
- Pin a branded headline to Position 2 (e.g., "Spavia Day Spa Plano")
- Leave Position 3 unpinned for Google to test
- Pin Description 1 to a lead description; leave Description 2 unpinned

## Part 5: Campaign Lifecycle — Keep, Reuse, or Rebuild?

### Short answer: Keep the campaign shell and reuse it. Swap creative seasonally.

### Why reuse beats rebuilding

1. **Campaign history matters.** Google's ad rank calculation incorporates historical campaign performance. A campaign with months of click and conversion data (even paused periods) gets a small quality advantage over a brand-new campaign starting from zero.

2. **Audience lists persist.** Your remarketing audiences, audience observations, and bid adjustments carry forward. Rebuilding means re-applying all of this.

3. **Negative keyword lists accumulate.** Every irrelevant search term you block over Mother's Day benefits Christmas. A new campaign starts with zero negatives.

4. **Ad group structure is reusable.** "Mother's Day Gift" becomes "Holiday Gift," "Massage Gift Card" stays the same, "Spa Gift Card" stays the same, "Spa Packages" stays the same. Only the occasion-specific ad group needs a creative swap.

5. **Extensions and sitelinks carry forward.** You'd have to rebuild all of these for a new campaign.

### How to manage the lifecycle

| Timeframe | Action |
|---|---|
| **May 12 (post-Mother's Day)** | Pause the campaign. Do NOT delete or remove anything. |
| **May 20** | Duplicate the "Mother's Day Gift" ad group. Rename the copy to "Father's Day Gift." Swap headlines/descriptions to Father's Day messaging. Pause the "Mother's Day Gift" ad group. Unpause "Father's Day Gift." Set budget to $8-12/day. Reactivate campaign. |
| **Jun 16 (post-Father's Day)** | Pause "Father's Day Gift" ad group. Leave Massage Gift Card, Spa Gift Card, Spa Packages ad groups enabled at $3-5/day as an evergreen gift card campaign through the summer. These keywords have year-round volume. |
| **Oct 15** | Create "Holiday Gift" ad group (clone Mother's Day Gift, swap copy). Rename campaign to `2026-Search - Gift Cards` (drop the Mother's Day suffix now that it's multi-season). Ramp budget per the seasonal calendar. |
| **Dec 26 (post-Holiday)** | Pause "Holiday Gift" ad group. Return to evergreen mode. |
| **Jan 5** | Create "Valentine's Gift" ad group. Same pattern. |
| **Apr 2027** | Reactivate "Mother's Day Gift" ad group. Refresh headlines/descriptions with 2027 dates. Repeat the cycle. |

### Rename the campaign

Since you're keeping it long-term, rename from `2026-Search - Gift Cards (Mother's Day)` to just:

**`2026-Search - Gift Cards`**

The seasonal specificity lives at the ad group level, not the campaign level. This avoids confusion when you're running Holiday ad groups inside a campaign called "Mother's Day."

### The Display campaign follows the same pattern

Pause the Display campaign between seasons. Swap creative assets when you reactivate. Same audiences, same targeting, new images and ad copy.

## Part 6: Landing Page Constraints and Workarounds

You can't change the `/gift-cards` page. It's franchisor-controlled and currently very thin (one headline, one CTA, no pricing, no imagery). This is why QS landing page experience is "Below average."

### What you CAN do

1. **Use the most specific URL path available.** The current URL is `https://planotx.spaviadayspa.com/gift-cards?utm_campaign=2026-mothers-day`. This is correct. The UTM parameter doesn't affect LP scoring.

2. **Mirror the LP language in your ad copy.** The gift card page says "Give the Gift of Relaxation." Use this exact phrase in at least one RSA headline or description. Google's LP relevance algorithm looks for text overlap between the ad and the page.

3. **Add sitelinks that offer LP depth.** Since the main LP is thin, your sitelinks to `/spa-packages`, `/massage`, `/pricing`, and `/services` give Google secondary signals that the overall site experience is substantive.

4. **Don't fight the LP score — outperform on CTR and ad relevance instead.** QS is multiplicative across three components. If LP experience is stuck at "Below average," you need CTR and ad relevance to be "Above average" to compensate. Strong RSAs with 15 distinct headlines and 4 descriptions are the lever.

5. **Flag to the franchisor (for the record):**
   - The `/gift-cards` page needs gift card denominations/options visible
   - It needs Mother's Day-specific messaging during the season (even a banner)
   - The 24-hour activation hold messaging may discourage last-minute buyers
   - No product images or pricing on the page tanks LP experience scores
